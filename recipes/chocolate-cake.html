Chocolate Cake

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups All-Purpose Flour(Mug)
  • 2 Cups Castor Sugar
  • 1/2 Cup Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
  • 6 Teaspoons Baking Powder
  • 6 Eggs
  • 1 Cup Boiling Water
  • 1 Cup Vegetable Oil
  • 1 Teaspoon Vanilla Extract

Instructions

Step 1: Bloom the Cocoa

In a small heatproof bowl or jug, whisk the 1/2 cup cocoa powder into the 1 cup boiling water until completely smooth and free of lumps. Set aside to cool slightly while you prepare the batter.

Step 2: Whip the Egg Base

In a large mixing bowl, beat the 6 eggs and 2 cups castor sugar together using an electric hand mixer or stand mixer on high speed for 5 to 7 minutes. The mixture should become thick, pale yellow, and significantly increased in volume.

Step 3: Stream in the Oil and Vanilla

Lower the mixer speed and slowly pour in the 1 cup oil and 1 tsp vanilla essence. Mix just until fully incorporated.

Step 4: Sift and Fold Dry Ingredients

Sift the 2 cups cake flour and 6 tsp baking powder together. Add this to the egg mixture in two batches, folding gently with a spatula or using the lowest speed on your mixer until the flour is just combined. Do not overmix.

Step 5: Add Chocolate Liquid

Gently pour the warm cocoa-water mixture into the batter. Fold slowly until you have a completely smooth, uniform consistency. The batter will be quite thin and runny—this is exactly how it should look.

Step 6: Bake

Divide the batter evenly between two greased and lined 9-inch (22cm) cake tins. Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for 25 to 30 minutes, or until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow to cool completely before icing.

Chef's Tip for the Blog: Remind your readers that because this batter is quite thin, it is crucial to line the bottom of the cake tins with baking paper (parchment paper) to prevent any leaks or sticking!
